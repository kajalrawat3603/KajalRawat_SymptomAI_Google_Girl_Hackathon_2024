Medical Forecasting

Medical forecasting uses data analysis and predictive modeling to anticipate healthcare trends, disease patterns, and resource demands. It supports proactive decision-making and efficient resource allocation, ultimately enhancing public health outcomes by predicting needs and preventing issues before they arise.

Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body either doesn't produce enough insulin or can't use it effectively, leading to high blood sugar levels.

It requires lifelong management through medication, diet, and exercise to prevent complications such as heart disease, stroke, and nerve damage.

Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's disease is a progressive neurological disorder characterized by tremors, stiffness, and impaired movement. It occurs due to the loss of dopamine-producing cells in the brain. While there's no cure, medications and therapies can help manage symptoms and improve quality of life.

Heart Disease

Heart disease refers to various conditions that affect the heart's function and blood vessels, including coronary artery disease, heart attacks, and heart failure.Risk factors include high blood pressure, high cholesterol, smoking, and obesity.

Symptoms include chest pain and shortness of breath.
